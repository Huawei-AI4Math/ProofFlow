Informal theorem and proof
Theorem: Prove that for any $a, b, c$ that are the side lengths of a triangle, the following inequality holds: $a^2 + b^2 + c^2 < 2(ab + bc + ca)$. Proof: Let $a, b, c > 0$ be the side lengths of a triangle. Then $2(ab+bc+ca) - (a^2+b^2+c^2) = (a+b-c)(a+c-b) + (a+b-c)(b+c-a) + (a+c-b)(b+c-a) > 0$ by the triangle inequalities. Hence, $a^2+b^2+c^2 < 2(ab+bc+ca)$.
