Informal theorem and proof
Theorem: Prove that for all positive real numbers $a, b, c$, the following inequality holds: $$\\frac{a^{2}}{b}+\frac{b^{2}}{c}+\frac{c^{2}}{a} \geq a+b+c$$ Proof: For all positive real numbers $a, b, c$, note that $(x-y)^2 \ge 0$ implies $x^2+y^2 \ge 2xy$, and dividing by $y>0$ gives $ rac{x^2}{y}+y \ge 2x$. Applying this to $(a,b), (b,c), (c,a)$ yields $ rac{a^2}{b}+b \ge 2a$, $ rac{b^2}{c}+c \ge 2b$, and $ rac{c^2}{a}+a \ge 2c$. Summing these inequalities gives $ rac{a^2}{b}+ rac{b^2}{c}+ rac{c^2}{a}+a+b+c \ge 2(a+b+c)$, and subtracting $a+b+c$ from both sides yields $ rac{a^2}{b}+ rac{b^2}{c}+ rac{c^2}{a} \ge a+b+c$.
