Informal theorem and proof
Theorem: \ithm{Mazur} Let~$E$ be an elliptic curve over~$\Q$. Then $E(\Q)_{ or}$ is isomorphic to one of the following 15 groups: \begin{align*} \zmod{n} & \qquad ext{ for } n\leq 10 ext{ or } n=12,\\ \Z/2\Z\cross \Z/2n &\qquad ext{ for } n \leq 4. \end{align*} Proof: We have \begin{eqnarray*} a^2+b^2&=&c^2\\ \frac{1}{2}ab&=&n \end{eqnarray*} Add or subtract $4$ times the second equation to the first to get \begin{eqnarray*} a^2\pm2ab +b^2&=&c^2\pm 4n\\ (a\pm b)^2 &=& c^2 \pm 4n\\ \left(\frac{a\pm b}{2}\right)^2 &=& \left( \frac{c}{2}\right)^2 \pm n \\ &=& A \pm n \end{eqnarray*}
