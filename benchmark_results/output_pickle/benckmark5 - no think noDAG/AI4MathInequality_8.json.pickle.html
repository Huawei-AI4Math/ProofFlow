Informal theorem and proof
Theorem: Prove that for all real numbers $x$, the following inequality holds: $2 x^{4} + 1 \geq 2 x^{3} + x^{2}$. Proof: For all real $x$, $2x^4 + 1 - 2x^3 - x^2 = 2x^4 - 2x^3 - x^2 + 1 = (x-1)^2(2x^2 + 2x + 1) \ge 0$, since $(x-1)^2 \ge 0$ and $2x^2 + 2x + 1 = 2(x+1/2)^2 + 1/2 > 0$. Therefore, $2x^4 + 1 \ge 2x^3 + x^2$ for all real $x$.
