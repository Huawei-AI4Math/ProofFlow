Informal theorem and proof
Theorem: Prove that for all real numbers $x$ and $y$, the following inequality holds: $x^{4} + y^{4} + 4xy + 2 \geq 0$. Proof: We want to show that $x^4 + y^4 + 4xy + 2 \ge 0$ for all real numbers $x$ and $y$. Observe that the expression can be rearranged and factored as $$x^4 + y^4 + 4xy + 2 = (x^4 - 2x^2y^2 + y^4) + (2x^2y^2 + 4xy + 2) = (x^2 - y^2)^2 + 2(xy + 1)^2$$. Since $(x^2 - y^2)^2 \ge 0$ and $(xy + 1)^2 \ge 0$ for all real $x$ and $y$, we have $2(xy + 1)^2 \ge 0$. Therefore, $$(x^2 - y^2)^2 + 2(xy + 1)^2 \ge 0$$, and thus $x^4 + y^4 + 4xy + 2 \ge 0$ for all real numbers $x$ and $y$.
